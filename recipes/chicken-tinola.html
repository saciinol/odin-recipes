Chicken Tinola

Description

Chicken Tinola is a classic Filipino stew–a soul-warming soup known and cherished across generations. This Allrecipes version features tender bone-in chicken simmered in a fragrant ginger-garlic-onion broth, brightened with fish sauce and enriched by wholesome veggies like chayote squash, bok choy, and spinach. The interplay of savory chicken, spicy warmth from fresh ginger, and crisp greens delivers a comforting, well-balanced taste that makes it perfect for any day—especially when you're in need of a light yet hearty meal.

Beyond its delicious flavor, Tinola carries cultural significance as one of the Philippines’ most beloved dishes, often served with rice and a side of fish sauce for added punch. Its clean, nourishing broth makes it a go-to when feeling under the weather, while its adaptable nature invites variations—substituting greens like moringa or chili leaves, swapping chayote for green papaya, or dialing up the ginger for extra zing. Whether for lunch, dinner, or a comforting remedy, this Chicken Tinola stands out as a timeless staple of Filipino home cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (1 1/2 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 3 pounds chicken legs and thighs, rinsed and patted dry
  • 2 (14 ounce) cans chicken broth
  • 1 chayote squash, peeled and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 head bok choy, chopped
  • 1 (8 ounce) package fresh spinach, chopped

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and garlic; cook and stir until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  3. Add ginger and fish sauce; cook and stir for 1 to 2 minutes. Stir in chicken and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Pour in chicken broth and cook for 5 minutes. Add squash and simmer until chicken is no longer pink in the center, about 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Add bok choy and spinach; cook until spinach is just wilted, 1 to 2 minutes. Serve hot.
