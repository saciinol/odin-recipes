Famous Chicken Adobo

Description

This Allrecipes "Famous Chicken Adobo" is a sumptuous Filipino classic featuring tender bone‑in chicken pieces simmered in a savory-tangy braise. The recipe starts by browning the chicken to lock in flavor, then continues with a rich blend of soy sauce, white vinegar, garlic powder, black pepper, and fragrant bay leaf—all cooked until the chicken is gloriously succulent in a sauce that’s both juicy and full‑bodied. Clocking in under an hour, it’s a weeknight hero: minimal prep, maximum flavor.

What makes this adobo stand out is its perfect balance of salty umami and bright acidity, a hallmark of the national dish of the Philippines. This version leans saucier than others—ideal for topping a steaming mound of white rice to soak up every drop . Like many Filipino adobo recipes, it shines even brighter the next day, as flavors meld and intensify overnight . It’s humble, deeply comforting, endlessly flexible—play with adding garlic cloves, peppercorns, or a swipe of sugar, and savor a dish rooted in centuries of culinary tradition and personal family preference.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 (3 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, quartered and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • ⅔ cup low sodium soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf

Steps

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken pieces until golden brown, 2 to 3 minutes per side. Transfer chicken to a plate and set aside.
  2. Add onion and garlic to the skillet; cook until softened and brown, about 3 to 5 minutes.
  3. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar and season with garlic powder, black pepper, and bay leaf.
  4. Return chicken to pan, increase heat to high, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until chicken is tender and cooked through, 35 to 40 minutes.
